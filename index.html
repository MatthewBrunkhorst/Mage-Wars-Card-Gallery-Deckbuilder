Searching is not case sensitive. You can search for multiple things at once by separating terms with commas (e.x. Flying, Demon). This will search for cards that contain at least one of the things searched for. For more advanced search options, you can use Advanced Search. Searching with a blank search bar (or reloading the page) will show all of the cards again. Searching with text in more than one search bar will search for cards that have at least one thing from each search bar. For example, putting "Flying, Demon" in the first bar and "Legendary, Burn" in the second bar will search for cards that contain either word Flying or the word Demon, and that also contain either the word Legendary or the word Burn. In other words, the search function treats the content of the search bars as: Find card that contains ((Flying OR demon) AND (legendary OR burn)).

Deckbuilding Menu
(hover for more info)
This is the menu for creating and editing a deck. Clicking on any card on the right adds it to the deck if there are not already the maximum possible copies in the deck. The menus below allow you to select a Mage, which affects the spellpoint cost of each card. It then displays and updates the spellpoint cost depending on the current mage and deck. Finally, it shows the current deck. Clicking any card in the deck from this list removes a copy of that card from the deck.
The way Spell Point Cost is calculated is based on the level of a card (found near the top right corner of each card) and the selected mage's training/opposition to its schools of magic. For instance, the default mage is Beastmaster, who is trained in Nature school spells but opposed to Fire school spells. Any spells they are trained in cost 1 point per level, spells they are opposed to cost 3 points per level, and other spells cost 2 points per level. For example, Arc Lightning is a Level 1 Air spell, so it costs 2 spell points. Fireball is a level 2 Fire spell, so it costs 6 spell points. Regrowth Belt is a level 1 Nature spell, so it costs 1 spell point.
